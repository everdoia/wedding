Our Story

Maggie moved to the Monterey Peninsula in 2006 after graduating from Gettysburg College, for two reasons: to be in the land of John Steinbeck and to get as far away from the East Coast as possible (what can I say, the girl loves adventure!). She joined an AmeriCorps program, drove across the country with her good friend Kris, moved into an apartment with two other girls — one from Oregon, and one from Washington — and her new life had begun!

Evan grew up in the San Francisco Bay area and was attending CSU Monterey Bay. After enduring a few debaucl-ey housing situations, he responded to an advertisement on Craigslist for an apartment already occupied by two AmeriCorps members, who were looking for a third roommate. Lo and behold, these two AmeriCorps members (Michele and Jeremy) were in the same program as Maggie! They were, and still are, great friends and will be representing in the wedding party!

After being introduced by Michele, Maggie and Evan hit it off and the rest is history!

The Memory Gardens Monterey, CA

The wedding and reception will be held at Memory Gardens in Monterey, CA, which is the most visited garden in Monterey State Historic Park. Graceful stucco archways, framed by multi-colored roses and other flowers, make this a photographer's favorite. Stucco walls of the Pacific House and garden isolate this garden from the city, creating quiet and solitude. Located at the back of the Pacific House Museum at Custom House Plaza, Memory Gardens is within walking distance of downtown Monterey, the beach, Fisherman's Wharf, and several bars and restaurants.

Airports

You have two options: 1. San Jose International Airport (KSJC) or Monterey Regional Airport (KMRY).

San Jose Airport is about an hour and a half's drive away from Monterey, but is usually the cheaper option. Monterey Airport, although only a 15 minute drive from downtown, can sometimes be more expensive. We are in the process of negotiating bulk flight prices with airlines. Driving From the Airport

Highway 17 (From SJC Airport)

This is a really windy road with beautiful views, but not for the faint of heart. Take this road in a small car only. It is the fastest way to get to Monterey from San Jose Airport . . .

Highway 101 to Highway 1 (From SJC Airport)

This route is just as good and much more "highway." You can take this directly into Monterey and ride along Highway 1, overlooking the ocean (Maggie's commute for a few years!).

Highway 68 (From MRY Airport)

This is a short ride and a beautiful drive from the airport to downtown Monterey.

Public Trolley Free

If you stay in Monterey, there is a free trolley that runs throughout most of Monterey and adjoining cities for free! The trolley runs every 15 minutes. You can find out more information on the MST website here: Monterey Trolley

Adventures By The Sea

Adventures by the Sea is our favorite outdoor company in Monterey! They have a lot of fun things to do, such as: kayaking, paddle boarding, biking, and tours of the area. If you want to use their services, call and mention the Wunderlich/Verdoia wedding to receive a discount.

Adventures By The Sea

Dennis the Menace Park Great for kids!

Imagine a free park with numerous slides, swings, a rock wall, and fun climby things. This is a place where your kids can run and burn off tons of energy, and you can slide and climb with the kids. This is the best place for photo ops! Did i mention it's FREE?!

17 Mile Drive Some of our favorite views

17 Mile Drive is a beautiful, scenic drive that overlooks the coastline and winds between million dollar homes and the Pebble Beach golf course. The Lone Cypress tree, a Monterey landmark, is really cool around dusk. It does cost to enter the drive, but the fee ($9.75) can be reimbursed if you dine at one of the Pebble Beach resort restaurants. If you are a golfer, you have to see the greens here!

Point Lobos State Reserve

The California coast is so beautiful and rugged here, and the Monterey cypress grove juxtaposed against the rocky coast and the vibrant blue ocean is equally stunning. This park is a favorite of Maggie and Evan's, as they spent a lot of time here when they lived on the peninsula. Definitely bring a camera, water, a hat, and some good walking shoes, because you'll be out there in the sun a long time walking the (well-marked, generally easy, a couple handicap accessible) trails admiring the beautiful coastal views.

Point Lobos

Monterey Bay Aquarium

A really cool place that's home to sea otters, penguins, sharks, jellies and thousands of other marine animals and plants. Evan once swam in the Outer Bay Tank as part of his summer job!

Pro tip:

Buy your tickets from your hotel, and get the tickets that are good for two days (so you can go the afternoon you arrive and again the next morning). The tickets are the same price as the regular tickets but give you much greater flexibility, especially if you have kids and will need to take a break in touring. Take time to visit one of the best aquariums in the United States!

Monterey Bay Aquarium

Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk

Old-fashioned carnival games and snack booths can be found throughout the 24-acre (9.7 ha) park. It is located at 400 Beach Street in Santa Cruz, south of the Ocean Street exit of California State Route 1, which is the southern terminus of California State Route 17. It has won the Best Seaside Amusement Park Award from Amusement Today in 2007, 2008, and 2009. Although there is no admission and the beach is public, parking is charged a fee when the rides are open. Season or day passes can be purchased or tickets for one dollar; rides take 3 to 5 tickets each.

Here are some of Evan's favorite golf courses in the area:

Del Monte Golf Course

The 18-hole "Del Monte" course at the Del Monte Golf Course facility in Monterey, California features 6,357 yards of golf from the longest tees for a par of 72 . The course rating is 71.6 and it has a slope rating of 125 on Blue grass. Designed by Charles Maud, the Del Monte golf course opened in 1897. Pebble Beach Resorts manages this facility, with Neil Allen as the Head PGA Professional/ General Manager.

Link

Bayonet/Black Horse Golf Course

The 18-hole "Bayonet" course at the Bayonet/Black Horse Golf Course facility in Seaside, California features 7,094 yards of golf from the longest tees for a par of 72 . The course rating is 75.1 and it has a slope rating of 139. Designed by Major General Robert McClure/(R)Gene Bates, the Bayonet golf course opened in 2007. Richard Fitzgerald manages the course as the General Manager.

Link

Monterey Pines Golf Course

The 18-hole "Monterey Pines" course at the Monterey Pines Golf Course facility in Monterey, California features 5,554 yards of golf from the longest tees for a par of 69 . Designed by Robert Muir Graves/(R)Kevin Tucker/Marc Messier, the Monterey Pines golf course opened in 2009. Navy Golf Program manages this facility, with Sam Jepsen as the Head Golf Professional.
